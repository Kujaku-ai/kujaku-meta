Buttons 紋 · mon

Button.

Pressed, not clicked. Every button raises at rest and debosses on hover — a tactile echo of the paper brand. Slash-prefix indicates route navigation; plain labels indicate action. The deboss is the affordance.

Surface modifiers

Six treatments. Default paper, primary red, ink coal, oxblood ceremonial, outline ghost, and on-dark for nested placement.

Six surfaceshover each to feel the press

Size ladder

Three scales. Padding, font-size, and shadow offsets step together. The press mechanic scales with them.

.is-sm · default · .is-lgsame surface across three sizes

CLASSES .btn.is-primary.is-sm (10px / 8·18 padding) · default (11px / 12·26) · .btn.is-primary.is-lg (13px / 16·34).

Route semantics

Slash prefix means the button navigates. No slash means the button acts. The ::before pseudo supplies the slash; the label text does not include it.

Action vs routeside-by-side

vs subscribe

Left: .btn performs. Right: .btn.is-route navigates. Markup text for the right button is subscribe — the slash is drawn by the pseudo-element.

Route variantsacross surfaces and sizes

open-position about btc methodology

CLASSES .btn.is-route composes with any surface modifier (primary, outline) and any size modifier (.is-sm). Slash colour tunes per surface.

Disabled state

Brand's disabled vocabulary. --ink-pale text, flat shadow, cursor: not-allowed. No red-X, no strikethrough. Applies to every button surface; future cycles may extend to other primitives.

Enabled vs disabled.btn.is-primary

Left: enabled. Middle: disabled attribute. Right: [aria-disabled="true"]. Both states render identically — ARIA path is for anchor-based buttons.

On-dark context

Inside dark cards, .is-on-dark replaces surface-filled variants. Press grammar moves from shadow-stack to border + translucent fill — the light-on-light letterpress illusion cannot reach across warm-dark paper.
