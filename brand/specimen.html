Route semantics

Slash prefix means the button navigates. No slash means the button acts. The ::before pseudo supplies the slash; the label text does not include it.

vs subscribe

Left: .btn performs. Right: .btn.is-route navigates. Markup text for the right button is subscribe — the slash is drawn by the pseudo-element.

CLASSES .btn.is-route composes with any surface modifier (primary, outline) and any size modifier (.is-sm). Slash colour tunes per surface.

Disabled state

Brand's disabled vocabulary. --ink-pale text, flat shadow, cursor: not-allowed. No red-X, no strikethrough. Applies to every button surface; future cycles may extend to other primitives.

Enabled vs disabled.btn.is-primary

Left: enabled. Middle: disabled attribute. Right: [aria-disabled="true"]. Both states render identically — ARIA path is for anchor-based buttons.

On-dark context

Inside dark cards, .is-dark replaces surface-filled variants. Press grammar moves from shadow-stack to border + translucent fill — the light-on-light letterpress illusion cannot reach across warm-dark paper.

Our sizing rule is boring on purpose. A position earns a weight based on its own volatility and the conviction score it holds in our model — and the three biggest holdings are capped collectively at 60%. The result is a book that would be unremarkable on a good week and still readable on a bad one.

Here is how our current sizing compares to what a typical sector-rotation fund runs, averaged across the last eight quarterly filings. The differences are not dramatic; they're small choices that compound.

We hold fewer things, for longer, with more weight on the names we actually believe. It is not a clever strategy — it's a slow one. Calm observation is the whole method.
